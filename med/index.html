Diabetes

It happens when insulin is low or not used effectively. Common symptoms include excessive thirst, frequent urination, fatigue, and blurred vision. Blood Pressure

Blood Pressure (BP) is the force of blood pushing against the walls of your arteries as your heart pumps, measured in millimeters of mercury (mmHg) and recorded as systolic/diastolic (e.g., 120/80 mmHg). High BP (hypertension) or low BP (hypotension) can cause serious health problems. To maintain healthy BP, eat a balanced diet low in salt and processed foods, exercise regularly, avoid smoking and limit alcohol, monitor your BP frequently, manage stress effectively, and take medications as prescribed when needed.

Heart Disease

Heart Disease refers to various conditions that affect the heart, including coronary artery disease, heart attacks, and heart failure. It is often caused by blocked arteries, high blood pressure, high cholesterol, or lifestyle factors. To reduce risk, maintain a healthy diet low in saturated fats and sugar, exercise regularly, avoid smoking and limit alcohol, manage stress, monitor blood pressure and cholesterol, and follow medical advice or medications as prescribed..

Heart Disease

Asthma

Asthma is a chronic respiratory condition in which the airways become inflamed and narrowed, causing difficulty in breathing, wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath. To manage asthma and reduce attacks, avoid triggers like dust, smoke, and allergens, take prescribed medications or inhalers regularly, exercise moderately, maintain good indoor air quality, and monitor your symptoms to seek timely medical help when needed.

Asthma

Arthritis

Arthritis is a condition that causes inflammation and stiffness in the joints, leading to pain, swelling, and reduced mobility. To manage arthritis and protect your joints, maintain a healthy weight, exercise regularly with low-impact activities, eat an anti-inflammatory diet rich in fruits and vegetables, avoid repetitive strain on joints, take prescribed medications as directed, and consult your doctor for regular check-ups..

Arthritis

Cancer

Cancer is a group of diseases in which abnormal cells grow uncontrollably, potentially spreading to other parts of the body. Risk factors include smoking, poor diet, excessive alcohol, infections, and genetic predisposition. To reduce risk and support early detection, maintain a healthy lifestyle with a balanced diet and regular exercise, avoid tobacco and limit alcohol, protect yourself from infections and harmful sun exposure, attend regular health screenings, and follow medical advice promptly if any symptoms appear..
